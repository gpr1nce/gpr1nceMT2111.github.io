I am not a new developer, but a good bootcamp has me thinking in new ways.


Currently in MicroTrain's Full Stack / Hybrid Mobile App Dev course, I've been astonished at how powerful client side tools have become since I studied HTML v2.


It is as if I came home after a long day on the job building buttons for websites, parked my Saturn, and woke up the next morning with a Tesla in the driveway.


Today, ambition in Web design can vault over technical debt to work with effects that are routinely made available in specialized sites. Type F12 on a web page and there is the code waiting for you to learn from.


It's a design world both more demanding and more generous than ever I imagined.

Consider it an asset that I'm not transfixed by social media, even while striving to make a living in (with?) it (them?).


Not for me the hazards of the bleeding edge. I may be aquainted with the latest tools, but as a user I will find any interface hole and fall into it. This makes me a fine tester, but not so likely to jump for the latest.


I'm more inclined to ask why a particular app or site is compelling, but once persuaded that an app is worth putting on my device, then I am both tester and advocate.


Perhaps, as with GIT, social media make sense by diving in.
