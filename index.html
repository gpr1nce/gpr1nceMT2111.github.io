Hang in there until the light dawns

Point taken.


I am not a new web developer, but a good bootcamp has me thinking that way.


Currently in MicroTrain's Full Stack / Hybrid Mobile App Dev course, I'm been astonished at how powerful client side tools have become since I studied HTML v2.


It is as if I went to sleep one night after a day on the job building buttons for websites, parked my Pinto, and woke up the next morning with a Tesla in the driveway.


Today, ambition in Web design can vault over technical debt to work with effects that are routinely made available in specialized sites. Type F12 on a web page and there is the code waiting for you to learn from.


It's a design world both more demanding and more generous than ever I imagined.

Observation


Not for me the hazards of the bleeding edge. I may be aquainted with the latest tools, but as a user I will find any interface hole and fall into it. This makes me a fine tester, but not so likely to jump for the latest.


I consider it an asset that I'm not transfixed by social media, even while striving to make a living in it (them?).


Just as in the days of appointment broadcast TV I wonder how smartphone people find the time to be everywhere all at once.


I'm more inclined to ask why a particular app or site is compelling, but if you can persuade me that an app is worth putting on my device, then we'll really have something.


Perhaps, as with GIT, it's not so much understanding as diving in.
